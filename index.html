Evaluating current clinical practices in the prescription of Softband Bone Conduction Hearing Aids for children with CHL.

By Bethan Thomas

Abstract

Objectives: To evaluate prescribing practices for non-implantable Softband Bone Conduction Hearing Aids (SBCHAs) in children with conductive hearing loss (CHL) within a single Health Board.

  • Examined referral pathways, HL severity, and clinical justifications
  • Assessed consistency in the absence of formal guidelines

Methods: Retrospective review of clinical records from Oct 2019â€“Oct 2024 across four hospitals (n = 28 children).

  • Collected data: age at fitting, HL type/severity, medical conditions, clinical reasoning, ENT referrals

Results:

  • Significant variation in prescribing practices
  • Unclear clinical justifications and inconsistent ENT referrals
  • Inconsistent documentation of management alternatives

Limitations: Retrospective design, small sample, incomplete records and outcome data.

Conclusion: SBCHA prescribing practices lack consistency and clear documentation. Local guidelines and structured documentation are recommended. A decision-making framework (Appendix A) was developed.
